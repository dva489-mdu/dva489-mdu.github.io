Cookie integrity attacks

Some of the NoSQL injection attacks relied on editing the session cookie. Even though cookies originate from the server we cannot trust them unless extra precautions are made to prevent tampering.

The attack

In Squeak! no extra measures ensure the cookie integrity are taken. As a result, the client can freely modify the cookie without the application noticing. While the NoSQL attacks that relied on cookie modification have been removed, at least two session integrity attacks remain. Your first task is to demonstrate

  1. how to impersonate another user by posting in his name, and
  2. how to access another user's private messages

by tampering with the cookie.

Protecting the application

To protect the cookie from tampering we will add a digital signature to the cookie information. Before trusting the cookie the server must verify the signature. Failed verification indicates that the cookie has been tampered with, and should lead to a terminated connection. See, e.g., Securing Web Cookies With Signatures for information on how to use signatures to protect cookies from tampering.
